Beyond the pavilion long, Beside the ancient way, The fragrant grass spreads green, reaching up to the sky

Thoughts in the Silent Night Before my bed a pool of light, I wonder if it's frost upon the ground. I raise my head and look at the bright moon, Then bow my head and think of my far-off hometown..

Looking on Spring in the Capital Though a country be sundered, hills and rivers endure; And spring comes green again to trees and grasses there. In grief for the times, even flowers bring tears to my eyes; Hating to be parted, hearing birds alarms my heart. The beacon fires have been burning for three months on end; A letter from home is worth a fortune in gold. My grizzled hair grows even shorter with all my worrying, Too thin to hold the hairpins any more.
